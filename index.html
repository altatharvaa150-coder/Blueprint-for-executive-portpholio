Educational Background

Harjeet Jaggi holds a Doctor of Philosophy (Ph.D.) and a Master of Technology (M.Tech.) from the Indian Institute of Technology, Delhi — one of India's foremost centres of scientific and engineering research. His doctoral work focused on the advanced study of materials science, encompassing polymer engineering and composite material behaviour.

His undergraduate foundation was laid at the Cochin University of Science & Technology (CUSAT), from which he graduated with a Bachelor of Technology (B.Tech.) in 2009 — beginning a trajectory that would carry him to the pinnacle of India's academic establishment.

Career

The Journey

2009 — 2011

Doctoral Research — IIT Delhi

Following the completion of his undergraduate studies, Harjeet Jaggi was admitted to IIT Delhi, where he pursued and completed both his M.Tech. and Ph.D. in materials science. This period of intensive research formed the intellectual foundation upon which his entire teaching practice is built — an understanding of science not merely as a subject to be taught, but as a discipline to be practised.

Faculty — Maharashtra

Maharashtra Institute of Technology, Pune

His first major teaching appointment was at the Maharashtra Institute of Technology (MIT), Pune, where he spent two years as faculty. Immersed in academia, he taught and mentored students of engineering and applied sciences — developing his pedagogical approach and learning to translate complex doctoral-level knowledge into clear, teachable concepts.

5 Years — Delhi

NEET Coaching Centre, Delhi

Seeking broader impact, he relocated to Delhi and joined a prominent NEET coaching centre, where he taught for five years. In this high-pressure, results-oriented environment, he refined his teaching methodologies — leveraging his understanding of Physics and Chemistry to help hundreds of aspiring medical students prepare for one of India's most competitive examinations.

Present — Pune

Founder — Teaching Institute, Kondhwa, Pune

Driven by a conviction that transformative education must be both rigorous and personal, Harjeet Jaggi founded his own teaching institute in Kondhwa, Pune. The institute has since produced students who have gained entry to esteemed medical and engineering colleges across the country. Both online and in-person modes of learning are offered.

Class Format

Modes of Learning

Both online and offline learning are supported, ensuring accessibility and flexibility for students from diverse locations and schedules.

💻

Online Classes

Live, interactive sessions conducted via Zoom or equivalent platforms. Sessions are not pre-recorded lectures — they are real-time, two-way interactions that mirror the depth and rigour of in-person teaching. Digital whiteboards and shared PDF materials are used to ensure concepts are reinforced visually.

🏠

Student's Home

Home visit sessions are available for students within Pune. This arrangement provides a comfortable, familiar learning environment and removes the need for travel on the student's part — particularly suitable for younger students or those with constraints on their schedule.

🏛️

Tutor's Institute

Classes at the Kondhwa, Pune teaching centre offer a dedicated learning environment equipped with whiteboard facilities, printed materials, and the atmosphere of a focused academic setting. Group and individual sessions are both available.

Methodology

How Sessions Are Conducted

01

Concept-First Approach

Every topic begins with foundational theory, ensuring that students understand the principles behind a formula before applying it. This builds genuine comprehension rather than surface memorisation.

02

Whiteboard & Visual Instruction

Complex problems in mechanics, thermodynamics, and electromagnetism are solved step-by-step on a whiteboard — allowing students to follow the reasoning process in real time, not merely the answer.

03

Structured PDF Materials

Detailed, well-organised PDF notes and problem sets are shared with students after each session, providing a reliable reference and enabling self-directed revision at their own pace.

04

Regular Assessment & Feedback

Progress is monitored through regular problem-solving exercises and assessments. Feedback is specific, constructive, and aimed at strengthening areas of weakness without undermining student confidence.

Structured PDF Notes

Every topic covered in class is followed by a two-page summary PDF — formulae, solved examples, and common exam traps — shared with the student after each session.

Live Problem Solving

Problems are never "explained" — they are solved live in front of the student, with every step narrated aloud. Students are then asked to reproduce the solution independently before the session ends.
