Class Format

Modes of Learning

Both online and offline learning are supported, ensuring accessibility and flexibility for students from diverse locations and schedules.

💻

Online Classes

Live, interactive sessions conducted via Zoom or equivalent platforms. Sessions are not pre-recorded lectures — they are real-time, two-way interactions that mirror the depth and rigour of in-person teaching. Digital whiteboards and shared PDF materials are used to ensure concepts are reinforced visually.

🏠

Student's Home

Home visit sessions are available for students within Pune. This arrangement provides a comfortable, familiar learning environment and removes the need for travel on the student's part — particularly suitable for younger students or those with constraints on their schedule.

🏛️

Tutor's Institute

Classes at the Kondhwa, Pune teaching centre offer a dedicated learning environment equipped with whiteboard facilities, printed materials, and the atmosphere of a focused academic setting. Group and individual sessions are both available.

Methodology

How Sessions Are Conducted

01

Concept-First Approach

Every topic begins with foundational theory, ensuring that students understand the principles behind a formula before applying it. This builds genuine comprehension rather than surface memorisation.

02

Whiteboard & Visual Instruction

Complex problems in mechanics, thermodynamics, and electromagnetism are solved step-by-step on a whiteboard — allowing students to follow the reasoning process in real time, not merely the answer.

03

Structured PDF Materials

Detailed, well-organised PDF notes and problem sets are shared with students after each session, providing a reliable reference and enabling self-directed revision at their own pace.

04

Regular Assessment & Feedback

Progress is monitored through regular problem-solving exercises and assessments. Feedback is specific, constructive, and aimed at strengthening areas of weakness without undermining student confidence.

Structured PDF Notes

Every topic covered in class is followed by a two-page summary PDF — formulae, solved examples, and common exam traps — shared with the student after each session.

Live Problem Solving

Problems are never "explained" — they are solved live in front of the student, with every step narrated aloud. Students are then asked to reproduce the solution independently before the session ends.
